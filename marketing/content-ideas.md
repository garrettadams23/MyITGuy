# Content Research Pass — June 2026

Recurring research task from `plan.md` Phase 14: periodically check where
IT-help-seekers actually ask questions and what they search for, and turn
that into a ranked shortlist of content/FAQ topics worth writing. This is a
shortlist to review, not a commitment to publish anything yet — there's no
blog/FAQ section in this repo today, so "publish" would mean either adding a
lightweight FAQ section to `index.html`/`studies.html` or starting a blog,
which is a separate decision.

**Methodology note**: Reddit (r/techsupport, r/sysadmin, r/smallbusiness)
blocks Anthropic's web-search crawler at the infrastructure level — the same
kind of server-side block hit earlier with the Notion source page for this
phase — so this pass couldn't pull live thread titles directly. Instead it
leans on aggregated 2026 small-business-IT-pain-point research, the
competitive landscape of DC-area IT/cybersecurity providers, and the
durable, evergreen "how do I fix X" questions that drive support search
traffic regardless of year. Worth re-running with direct Reddit access if
that ever becomes available.

## Ranked shortlist

1. **"Why Is My Computer So Slow? A No-Nonsense Troubleshooting Checklist"**
   — *Troubleshooting Systems*. One of the highest-volume, most universal IT
   questions there is, across Windows®, Android, and Linux alike. Cheap to
   write, broad reach, directly matches the quiz's "Troubleshooting Systems"
   result. Good first pick.

2. **"Backup and Disaster Recovery on a Small-Business Budget"**
   — *Software Solutions*. Multiple 2026 industry sources (Omega Systems,
   TrueITPro, Charlotte IT Solutions) independently flag backup/disaster
   recovery as the top IT pain point for small businesses this year —
   ransomware and cloud misconfiguration risk keep pushing it up the list.

3. **"CMMC, DFARS, and ITAR, Explained for DC-Area Small Businesses"**
   — *Software Solutions*. The DC metro has an unusually high density of
   federal contractors/subcontractors. Every local competitor surveyed
   (OSIbeyond, Shipshape IT, Envescent) leads with compliance-framework
   language, which signals this confusion is a real, recurring local
   question — and it's a natural fit for Garrett's DoD background.

4. **"ServiceNow Dashboards 101: Building a Report Your Manager Will Actually Look At"**
   — *Excel & ServiceNow®*. Maps directly to one of the three service
   categories and to genuinely common "how do I build a dashboard/report"
   searches.

5. **"5 Excel Tricks Every Office Manager Should Know for Tracking Anything"**
   — *Excel & ServiceNow®*. Broad appeal, low effort, complements the quiz's
   "Excel & ServiceNow®" result with something concretely useful to skim.

6. **"Android Won't Connect to Wi-Fi? Try This Before You Call Anyone"**
   — *Troubleshooting Systems*. Classic, extremely common, low-effort FAQ
   entry — good for quick wins / search traffic rather than depth.

7. **"Zero Trust for Small Businesses: What It Actually Means (No Buzzwords)"**
   — *Software Solutions*. "Zero-trust infrastructure" is already in
   Garrett's own services copy; a plain-English explainer for non-technical
   small-business owners could become a differentiator against larger MSPs
   that assume the reader already knows the jargon.

8. **"Windows® vs. Linux for an Old Laptop You Don't Want to Throw Away"**
   — *Troubleshooting Systems*. Evergreen, approachable, and skews toward
   home users specifically — a distinct audience from the more B2B-leaning
   items above (#2, #3, #7).

## Recommendation

Start with #1, #2, and #4 — one from each of the three service categories,
all cheap to produce, all with a clear research signal behind them. Revisit
this list monthly (or whenever Garrett has bandwidth to write) and re-rank
based on what's actually converting once a couple of these are live.
